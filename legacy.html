The Spark of Revolution

1892-1896

Upon returning to the Philippines in 1892, Rizal founded the peaceful reform society La Liga Filipina. However, Spanish authorities, fearing his influence, exiled him to Dapitan in Mindanao (1892-1896).

Despite this, Rizal's writings continued to inspire Filipinos, ultimately contributing to the outbreak of the Philippine Revolution in 1896. In Dapitan, he fell in love withÂ Josephine Bracken, an Irish woman with whom he planned to build a life. However, their plans were tragically cut short.

A Symbol of Hope

December 30, 1896

Falsely accused of rebellion, Rizal was executed by firing squad on December 30, 1896, in Manila. His death became a rallying cry for the revolutionaries, further fueling the fight for independence.
